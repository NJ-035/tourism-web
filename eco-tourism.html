Explore Tarlac

ECO-TOURISM

Nestled in the heart of Central Luzon, Tarlac Province is a hidden gem for eco-tourism enthusiasts, offering a blend of lush landscapes and rich biodiversity. With its rolling hills, vibrant rice fields, and serene lakes, Tarlac invites travelers to immerse themselves in nature's tranquility. The province is home to picturesque eco-parks including Jose V. Yap Sports and Recreation Complex (former Tarlac Recreational Park),

Jose V. Yap Sports and Recreational Complex

San Jose, Tarlac

Jose V. Yap Sports and Recreational Complex - former Tarlac Recreational Park is named in honor of Jose V. Yap, a visionary leader and advocate for sports and community development, this state-of-the-art complex was built to foster physical well-being and promote unity. Opened to the public as a hub for athletes and families alike, it has since become a symbol of progress and excellence, offering a space for both competitive sports and recreational activities.


At the Jose V Yap Sports and Recreational Complex, there’s never a dull moment! Dive into action with a variety of sports—play basketball on the courts, hit the track for a jog, or serve up some fun with tennis, golf, and ATV. Families can enjoy peaceful picnics in green spaces or take a leisurely stroll around the scenic grounds. Whether you’re looking to compete, relax, or simply stay active, this is the place to be for fun, fitness, and endless possibilities!

Where passion meets play – the Jose V Yap Sports and Recreational Complex is your ultimate destination for fitness, fun, and friendly competition.

Capas National Shrine

Barangay Aranguren, Capas, Tarlac

Standing as a solemn tribute to the bravery and sacrifice of Filipino and American soldiers during World War II, the Capas National Shrine commemorates the harrowing history of the Death March. Located in Capas, Tarlac, it honors those who endured unimaginable hardship and those who perished along the march. The shrine features a majestic monument, as well as peaceful grounds where visitors can reflect on the courage and resilience of the fallen heroes. The site serves as a poignant reminder of the sacrifices made for freedom and peace.


In its center, there is a three-pronged pillar terminating into a sharpened peak. A seventy-meter concrete obelisk soaring above the trees and pointing to the great blue sky.


The shrine also features beautifully landscaped gardens, walking paths, and informative displays about the historical events of the Bataan Death March. Notably, the site includes a museum where artifacts and exhibits help tell the story of the courageous soldiers who endured the march. The peaceful atmosphere of the grounds invites visitors to pay their respects while learning about one of the darkest yet most important chapters in Philippine history."

It is more than just a memorial — it's a place of reflection and remembrance. The Shrine is raw. It is memorial to our fallen heroes. A place for their families and kin to touch their names on that black marble wall and remember.

Canding Falls

Barangay Maasin, San Clemente, Tarlac

At Canding Falls, adventure meets serenity. You can take a dip in the cool, clear waters at the base of the falls or enjoy a refreshing shower as the water cascades down from the rocks above. For those feeling more adventurous, the short trek to the falls offers a rewarding experience, as you pass through lush forests, listen to the sounds of nature, and take in breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. The falls' surroundings are dotted with vibrant greenery, offering perfect spots for picnics, photography, or simply unwinding by the water. If you’re looking to connect with nature, this hidden paradise gives you a chance to relax, swim, explore, and enjoy the peaceful, scenic beauty of the area.

Canding Falls offers a serene escape with its crystal-clear waters and lush surroundings. Perfect for swimming, hiking, and relaxing in nature’s embrace, this hidden gem promises a refreshing experience for all who visit.

To get to the waterfall, you need to hike a quite challenging route. Hence, it’s better for you to get some exercises first before you go here – otherwise you’ll be easily exhausted. The secluded location is making Canding Falls perfect to get some me-time in tranquility. If you want, you can swim and relax in the water, feel the cool waters hugging your body.

Monasterio De Tarlac

San Jose, Tarlac, Philippines

The Monasterio de Tarlac is a Benedictine monastery that was established in 2004. It serves as a peaceful spiritual retreat and pilgrimage site, notably housing a Relic of the True Cross, a fragment believed to be from the cross of Jesus Christ. The monastery's simple yet striking architecture blends with its natural surroundings, offering visitors a place for reflection and faith. It stands as a symbol of the Philippines' Catholic heritage and devotion.

At the Monasterio de Tarlac, visitors can enjoy a peaceful blend of spiritual reflection and natural beauty. One of the main highlights is the Relic of the True Cross, which pilgrims can venerate, adding a deeply spiritual dimension to their visit. The monastery offers a serene environment for prayer, meditation, and quiet contemplation, allowing guests to reconnect with their faith. It provides stunning panoramic views of Tarlac’s lush landscape, with designated viewpoints perfect for photography or simply taking in the tranquility of the area.

Sitio Tala: Little Baguio

Sitio Tala, Brgy. Burgos, Tarlac

Sitio tala, also known as "Little Baguio" is a scenic destination renowned for its cool climate and lush, mountainous landscape. Surely, it provides a refreshing experience, in contrast to the typical hot and dry weather found in most parts of the country. This area is often referred to as "Little Baguio" due to its similarity to Baguio City in terms of its climate and natural beauty.

Here, you can enjoy scenic drives, beautiful spots for photography, and cooler temperatures, which make it an ideal escape for those looking to enjoy nature and a serene atmosphere. If you want, you can go camping too!

Visitors to Sitio Tala can enjoy various activities, such as light hiking, exploring its peaceful trails, and taking in the panoramic views of Tarlac’s landscapes. The calm environment makes it ideal for outdoor meditation, picnics, or simply unwinding in nature’s embrace.

Where nature’s beauty meets tranquility, offering a peaceful escape to reflect, recharge, and reconnect

Lubigan Picnic Ground

Lubigan, San Jose, Tarlac, Philippines

Dueg-Kapya Ken Talna, also known as Dueg-Kapya, is renowned for its serene landscape, featuring a combination of lush green hills, clear waters, and amazing views. The name "Dueg-Kapya Ken Talna" translates to "cool water" in the local Ilocano dialect, reflecting the refreshing ambiance you will feel when you’re in the area.

This destination is ideal for picnicking, hiking, and enjoying nature, making it a favored spot for local families and visitors seeking a relaxing day out. Charge your camera (or smartphone) first so you can take a lot of nature pictures here!


Overall, t’s also a great place for families and groups to relax, with designated areas for grilling and simple outdoor recreation. To fully enjoy the area, visitors can bring food, set up a picnic, and explore nearby natural attractions, making it a perfect getaway for a day trip.

Bulsa River

San Jose, Tarlac

Bulsa River is a serene and scenic river, popular in Tarlac for its natural beauty and tranquil environment. It provides a peaceful ambience and allows you to connect more with nature. It offers a serene escape for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike. Known for its crystal-clear waters and calm surroundings, it offers a perfect setting for a relaxing day immersed in nature. The river's serene environment makes it an ideal spot for leisurely walks, photography, or simply unwinding by the water.

The one thing to do in Tarlac when you’re in Bulsa River is kayaking! Yup, it’s another attraction for an adrenaline junkie. Surrounded by the Zambales mountain range, you can kayak on the white water of Bulsa River.

No need to be worried about safety too, because the Rapid Stream Ecotours. Even if you’re a beginner, the rapids of Bulsa River are perfect for you. It’s not too fast but not too gentle either
